# Chapter 2: The Road

They walked together when the rain thinned, saying little. The road climbed, then fell, then climbed
again, as roads do when they were laid by people who trusted the land more than the map.

*"Where are you going?"* Elen asked, at last.

"Nowhere in particular," the stranger said. "I find that *nowhere* is easier to reach than most
places, and rarely disappoints."

It was the kind of answer that could mean everything or nothing. Elen decided, for the length of an
afternoon, to let it mean everything.

By dusk the clouds had broken, and the wet road caught the last of the light and gave it back doubled.
