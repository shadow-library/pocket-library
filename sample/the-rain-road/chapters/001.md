# Chapter 1: Arrival

The rain had not stopped for three days. Elen watched the road from beneath the eaves, where the
water ran in bright threads and pooled in the ruts left by carts long gone.

She had waited here before. She would wait again. That was the shape of the road: **arrivals**, and
the long grey spaces between them.

![Rain on the empty road](../images/rain.png)

A figure resolved out of the downpour — slow, deliberate, unbothered by the weather.

> "You came," she said, though she had not meant to speak first.

The stranger only nodded, and shook the rain from a heavy coat.

There were, Elen thought, three things worth knowing about a person who walks through rain like that:

- they are not in a hurry
- they are not afraid of the cold
- they have somewhere they intend to be

She was, as it turned out, wrong about the third one.
